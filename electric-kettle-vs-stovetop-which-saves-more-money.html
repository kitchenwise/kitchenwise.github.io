Electric Kettle vs Stovetop: Which Saves More Money?

We compare energy efficiency, real‑world costs, and long‑term savings to see which option actually wins.

Heating water is one of the most common kitchen tasks. While stovetops have been the traditional option, electric kettles claim to be faster and cheaper. Here’s how they actually compare once energy costs are factored in.

Energy Efficiency Comparison

  • Electric kettle: ~80–90% energy efficiency
  • Gas stovetop: ~40–55% efficiency
  • Electric stovetop: ~65–70% efficiency

Real‑World Cost Example

Boiling 1 liter of water daily with an electric kettle typically costs less per month than using a stovetop, especially in regions where electricity prices are moderate and gas efficiency losses are high.

When a Stovetop Still Makes Sense

If you rarely boil water or already use the stove for cooking, a stovetop kettle can be sufficient. However, it rarely wins on speed or efficiency.

Final Verdict

For most households, an electric kettle saves more money over time thanks to faster boiling and higher energy efficiency.

A stovetop kettle is fine for occasional use—but if you boil water daily, electric is the smarter long‑term choice.
